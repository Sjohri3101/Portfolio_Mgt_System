What Makes the Best Resume Template?

  • An attractive header: Emphasizes your name and includes space for contact details and professional links.
  • Readability: A balance of white space to text ensures your resume is easy to read.
  • The right amount of space: Choose templates that fit your level of experience, with ample space for detailed entries.
  • Professional font styles and sizes: Use fonts that are easy to read and maintain a professional appearance.
  • Customizable: Templates should be easily adaptable to your needs within our builder tool.
  • The right file format: Download your resume in PDF or DOCX, as preferred by the employer.

Resume Template FAQs

Resume.io’s resume builder offers a free option, a seven-day trial option, and tiered pricing plans. Read more about using Resume.io for free here.

Employers are looking for resumes that quickly get to the point and show why a candidate has the exact qualifications to fill a role at their company. Customize your resume for each job you apply to. Learn more about customizing your resume here.

List as much relevant experience as you’ve obtained in the past 10 years. For students or recent grads, include at least three jobs. Remove less relevant positions as you progress in your career.

Keep your resume to one page in virtually all situations. Exceptions may apply for executive candidates or in countries outside the U.S. and Canada.

Avoid including personal information such as date of birth, age, marital status, or religion. A headshot is generally unnecessary except in specific industries.

It’s generally sufficient to include an email address and phone number. Adding a state or city is optional.

Each employment history entry should contain 4-5 bullet points, with a minimum of three entries to total at least 12-15 bullet points.

Employers appreciate resumes that are easy-to-read and customized for the job description. Templates are helpful but should be personalized for each application.

A great resume summary should include positive adjectives about your character, key accomplishments, and qualifications for the role.

List skills as a bullet point list, including both technical (hard) skills and personal traits (soft skills). Refer to the job description for priority skills.
