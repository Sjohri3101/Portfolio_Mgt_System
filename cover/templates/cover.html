Employers are looking for Cover Letter that quickly get to the point and show why a candidate has the exact qualifications to fill a role at their company. Customize your Cover Letter for each job you apply to. Learn more about customizing your Cover Letter here.

List as much relevant experience as you’ve obtained in the past 10 years. For students or recent grads, include at least three jobs. Remove less relevant positions as you progress in your career.

Keep your Cover Letter to one page in virtually all situations. Exceptions may apply for executive candidates or in countries outside the U.S. and Canada.

Avoid including personal information such as date of birth, age, marital status, or religion. A headshot is generally unnecessary except in specific industries.

It’s generally sufficient to include an email address and phone number. Adding a state or city is optional.

Each employment history entry should contain 4-5 bullet points, with a minimum of three entries to total at least 12-15 bullet points.

Employers appreciate Cover Letter that are easy-to-read and customized for the job description. Templates are helpful but should be personalized for each application.

A great cover summary should include positive adjectives about your character, key accomplishments, and qualifications for the role.

List skills as a bullet point list, including both technical (hard) skills and personal traits (soft skills). Refer to the job description for priority skills.
